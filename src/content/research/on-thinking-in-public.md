## The premise

Most of what I learn dies in private notebooks. This is a note about the
opposite habit: publishing the rough middle of the work, not just the clean end.

## Why it compounds

Public notes do three things private ones can't:

- They invite correction earlier, when it's cheap.
- They force a clarity that only an imagined reader demands.
- They leave a trail others can follow — and find you by.

## Open questions

- How much polish is *too* much before the note stops being honest?
- What's the right cadence — daily, weekly, when-it-clicks?
